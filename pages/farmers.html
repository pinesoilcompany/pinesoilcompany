Pines Oil Company produces off-road biodiesel from locally collected waste cooking oil. Our product is designed for agricultural and industrial non-road diesel applications.

Our Product

Off-Road Biodiesel Built
for Working Land

East Texas farms and ranches depend on diesel fuel to operate. Tractors, irrigation pumps, hay equipment, and generators all run on it. Pines Oil Company offers an off-road biodiesel produced right here in our region as an alternative for those non-road applications.

Our product is derived from waste cooking oil collected from restaurants in the Lindale and Van areas, then processed at our Athens, Texas facility. This is a domestically sourced, regionally produced fuel, not an imported product.

Off-road biodiesel is not subject to the same on-highway tax structure as pump diesel, making it a cost-relevant consideration for large-volume agricultural users. Contact us to discuss current pricing and availability.

What We Produce

Our off-road biodiesel is produced from used cooking oil feedstocks and processed through standard transesterification. The following provides a general product overview. Contact us for full specification documentation.

B100

Blend Grade

Our base product is B100, meaning it is 100 percent biodiesel by volume. It can be blended with petroleum diesel at various ratios depending on your equipment requirements and operating conditions.

Off-Road

Intended Application

This fuel is classified for off-road use only. It is appropriate for farm tractors, irrigation equipment, generators, skid steers, and other non-highway diesel machinery.

Local

Why Biodiesel

Considerations for
Agricultural Operations

  • Domestically Produced
    Biodiesel made from domestic feedstocks reduces dependence on imported petroleum. All of our feedstock and production is American.
  • Off-Road Tax Considerations
    Off-road fuel is subject to different tax treatment than highway diesel. For high-volume farm operations, this is a factor worth evaluating with your accountant.
  • High Lubricity
    Biodiesel is well known for its superior lubricity properties compared to ultra-low-sulfur petroleum diesel, which can benefit injection pump and fuel system components in older equipment.
  • Locally Sourced Supply Chain
    Purchasing from Pines Oil Company means your fuel dollar stays in East Texas. The feedstock came from local restaurants and the processing jobs are local jobs.
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use biodiesel in my existing diesel equipment?
Many diesel engines, particularly those manufactured before 2007, can run on biodiesel blends with little or no modification. Equipment manufactured more recently may have compatibility considerations. We recommend consulting your equipment manufacturer documentation and contacting us to discuss your specific situation before purchasing.
What is the difference between B100 and blended biodiesel?
B100 is pure biodiesel with no petroleum diesel blended in. A B20 blend, for example, would be 20 percent biodiesel and 80 percent petroleum diesel. Different blends perform differently in varying temperatures and equipment types. We can discuss what may be appropriate for your operation.
How does cold weather affect biodiesel performance?
Biodiesel has a higher cloud point than petroleum diesel, meaning it can begin to gel at lower temperatures. In East Texas winters, this is generally manageable, particularly with blended products. Proper storage and blending practices address most cold-weather concerns. Contact us for guidance specific to your situation.

Do you deliver, or do buyers pick up at the Athens facility?
We are able to discuss both delivery and facility pickup options depending on your location and volume. Contact us to determine what works best for your operation.
