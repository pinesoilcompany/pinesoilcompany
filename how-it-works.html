The Process

How It Works

From your first inquiry to final recycling, here is exactly how our process operates — straightforward and fully managed on our end.

Step by Step

The Collection & Recycling Process

01

Contact Us & Assess Your Needs

Reach out by phone or through our contact form. We will ask about your operation type, the volume of used oil you generate, how frequently you need collection, and your location. Based on this, we will recommend the appropriate service arrangement — whether that is a recurring schedule, a one-time collection, or an ongoing commercial contract.

There is no obligation at this stage. We simply want to understand what you need before making any recommendations.

02

Schedule a Pickup

Once we have agreed on a plan, we schedule your first collection at a time that works around your facility's operations. We arrive with all necessary pumping equipment, transfer containers, and safety materials. You do not need to provide any special equipment or preparation beyond ensuring access to your storage containers.

For recurring clients, we establish a set schedule and show up as agreed — no need to call each time unless your volume changes.

03

Collection & Documentation

Our technicians safely transfer your used oil into our certified transport containers. We record the volume, assess the oil grade and contamination level on-site, and complete all required waste transfer documentation. You receive a copy for your own compliance records.

We handle all the paperwork. You simply confirm the transfer and retain your copy. Our process meets all applicable state and EPA requirements for used oil handlers.

04

Processing & Recycling

Once collected, we transport your used oil to our processing facility. Depending on the feedstock, it is directed toward biodiesel production, industrial fuel blending, or re-refining for lubricant use. Every gallon is accounted for — nothing goes to a landfill, and nothing is improperly discharged.

The result is a closed-loop system where your waste becomes a resource, and your facility stays clean and compliant.
